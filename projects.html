Sparsity Applications for Hydraulic Fracture Monitoring

Currently investigating applications of sparsity and compressed sensing to improve the localization of microseismic events in high noise in collaboration with Professor Shuchin Aeron. In particular we exploit the seismimic signal sparsity in an overcomplete basis consisting of space time propagators. Exploiting this structure we have proposed a sparsity penalized algorithms and workflow for super resolution extraction of time overlapping multiple seismic events from single well data. More detail on our initial finding can be found in our poster and paper presented at IGARSS 2012.

Coffee and Kalman Filtering

It is common practice to use a weighing scale during the preparation of coffee as hot water is mixed with the ground coffee. The scale is used to measure the total amount of water added to the coffee grounds. In order to better facilitate consistent brewing it is desirable to measure not just volume but also flow-rate during the preparation. In collaboration with Luminaire Coffee, I designed and tested a Kalman filter for estimating flow rates of coffee using a simple scale. This project was used as a demo for Specialty Coffee Association of America (SCAA) 2012 but may become a product in the future. Provisional Patent granted March 2012. More information.

Past

Particle Swarm Optimization for estimating chemical reaction parameters

Professor Soha Hassoun and I are working on using particle swarm optimization to determine the roles and associated parameters of various metabolites in complex biological reactions. The process involves estimating the parameters several sets of coupled ordinary differential equations, resulting in a highly non-linear inverse problem. In particular, we are applying concepts from sparse signal processing to decrease the number of training datasets needed to produce a predictive biological model.
