Projects


Current

Bartender recommendations & Topic Modeling

I'm currently working with the good folks at On The Bar to implement and develop a bartender recommendation engine for their mobile app. The engine uses Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and collapsed Gibbs Sampling to infer a low dimensional space of user's bartender preferences. We then use this inferred topic model to predict which bartenders users will like based on their current activity. The engine will be included in the deployed app later this October.


Coffee and Kalman Filtering

It is common practice to use a weighing scale during the preparation of coffee as hot water is mixed with the ground coffee. The scale is used to measure the total amount of water added to the coffee grounds. In order to better facilitate consistent brewing it is desirable to measure not just volume but also flow-rate during the preparation. In collaboration with Luminaire Coffee, I designed and tested a Kalman filter for estimating flow rates of coffee using a simple scale. This project was used as a demo for Specialty Coffee Association of America (SCAA) 2012 but may become a product in the future. Provisional Patent granted March 2012. More information.




Past

Sparsity Applications for Hydraulic Fracture Monitoring

Investigated the applications of sparsity and compressed sensing to improve the localization of microseismic events in high noise in collaboration with Professor Shuchin Aeron. In particular we exploit the seismic signal sparsity in an over complete basis consisting of space time propagators. Exploiting this structure we have proposed a sparsity penalized algorithms and work-flow for super resolution extraction of time overlapping multiple seismic events from single well data.


Low-Rank Hyper-Spectral Imaging

Professor Shuchin Aeron and I are investigated the applications of low-rank and sparse peanalized techniques for denoising hyperspectral images and improving accuracy of hyperspectral reconstruction with a limited number of measurements or projections. Below are results of using one our developed techniques to reconstruct noisy data from noisy bands based on the low rank nature of the images.
