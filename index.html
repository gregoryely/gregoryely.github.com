About Me


Hello, I'm Gregory Tsiang Ely, an Electrical Engineering graduate student at Tufts university. My research is focused on novel applications of stochastic and sparse signal processing to geophysical problems. In particular, I'm working on new techniques to perform hydraulic fracturing monitoring using sparsity and compressed sensing in collaboration with professor Shuchin Aeron.

Previously I've worked on hearing and acoustic and audiology research at Boston University Biomedical Engineering department, data analysis algorithms for gravitational wave detection with the LIGO consortium, and several infrared remote sensing projects and radar tracking algorithms through MIT Lincoln Laboratory.

In September 2013 I will begin a Ph.D at MIT Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Science (EAPS) under Alison Malcolm.
